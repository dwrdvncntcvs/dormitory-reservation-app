Tenant Owner
Owner
Guidelines

Owner must have an account and get it verified through email in order for them to create and publish a dormitory.

In creating a dormitory, the owner should provide the requirements needed and get it verified through your profile that can be found in the upper right button.

You will receive an email if the documents provided are verified by the administrators. If your verification is rejected, you will also receive an email explaining why it is rejected.

Owners can find their dormitory on the map provided by simply clicking on the map.

Owners can add rooms by simply filling out the form that is required. Owners can create their property room information like room capacity, how many tenant are currently occupying the room, and prices. The prices they need to put on the system is per person.

If your dormitory's unfortunately rejected by the admins, you are allowed to send new sets of documents to validate your dormitory. Be reminded that your dormitory like names and ownership should be synced with your documents.

Owners can add images of their dormitories so that their target tenant will have atleast a glimpse on what they were getting or having from the said dormitory or apartments.

Owners can add amenities for the tenants to know what are the things that their property has that may help the owner of a dormitory in increasing the appeal of their property.

Owner can simply add a landmark near the said dormitory. One or more landmarks is acceptable. You should also name the landmark.

Owners can delete their dormitory or apartment information. Before deleting there will be a reminder.

Instructions are posted on the dormitory detail and owner’s will be notified whether their payments are verified or not by the administrators through an email notification. Payments will only be accepted through Gcash.

After setting up the dormitory, it can be published publicly and can be viewed by the possible tenants.

Pending - You’ll see here the tenants that want to reserve a dormitory. Owner can view the reservation details and decide whether to accept or reject the reservation. Message will be provided why the reservation is rejected.

Accepted - Once the owner accepts the reservation, The tenant’s reservation will be moved in the accepted section. The owner can view the details of the accepted reservation. Tenants should interact personally with the owner to be marked as an active tenant.

Active - Owner can view all the active tenants and their details in this section. Owner can remove the tenant if the tenant is no longer renting the dormitory.

Tenant
Guidelines

Tenants must have an account and get it verified through email in order for them to make reservations.

Tenants can find dormitories on the search bar. They can find dormitories through the name of the dormitory, location, nearby places and establishments.

After searching, lists of the dormitories and its location in the map will be shown.

On the dormitory tab in the navigation bar, tenants can filter dormitories whether it is a popular dormitory, based on gender and price and they can also see reserved dormitories.

Tenants can view details. On the details, tenants can view the owner, available rooms, amenities, prices and its location in the map. Tenants can also ask questions for further information regarding the dormitory.

Rooms that are available are shown and tenants can reserve up to 6 slots.

They can make reservations by simply clicking the reserve button. Note that the tenants should be verified first to make reservations.

After making a reservation, details will be shown on the right side wherein there is an option if the tenant wants to cancel the reservation.

Tenants will be put in the pending reservations of the owner and should reach out personally to the owner to confirm their reservation.

After the owner has accepted the reservation, they will be now an active tenant of the dormitory.

As an active tenant of the dormitory, they are now allowed to rate the dormitory. Rating is based by numbers, 5 being the highest and 1 is the lowest.
